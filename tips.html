Common Medicinal Plants with the Usage

Learn how to use common medicinal plants effectively.

Aloe Vera

How to Use:

  • Apply fresh aloe vera gel to burns and wounds for quick healing.
  • Use aloe vera gel to soothe sunburns and skin irritation.
  • Drink aloe vera juice to aid digestion and detoxify the body.

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

How to Use:

  • Drink tulsi tea to relieve stress and improve immunity.
  • Chew a few tulsi leaves to reduce coughing or congestion.
  • Use tulsi oil for massage to relieve muscle pain and inflammation.

Turmeric

How to Use:

  • Mix turmeric powder with milk for a warm, soothing drink (Golden Milk).
  • Apply turmeric paste on wounds or scars to reduce inflammation and promote healing.
  • Use turmeric in cooking for its anti-inflammatory benefits.

Peppermint

How to Use:

  • Drink peppermint tea to aid digestion and relieve headaches.
  • Apply peppermint oil on the forehead to reduce headache pain.
  • Use peppermint leaves in a bath to relax and relieve muscle pain.

Ginger

How to Use:

  • Boil fresh ginger in water to make ginger tea for nausea relief.
  • Use ginger powder in smoothies to promote healthy digestion.
  • Apply ginger oil on sore muscles for a natural pain relief massage.

Neem

How to Use:

  • Use neem oil to treat skin infections and acne.
  • Chew neem leaves to improve oral hygiene and freshen breath.
  • Boil neem leaves in water and use it as a natural pesticide in gardens.

Lavender

How to Use:

  • Use lavender oil in a diffuser for relaxation and better sleep.
  • Apply lavender oil to skin for its antiseptic properties to treat minor cuts or burns.
  • Make a calming lavender tea to reduce stress and anxiety.

Rosemary

How to Use:

  • Use rosemary oil for hair care to promote growth and reduce dandruff.
  • Drink rosemary tea to improve memory and cognitive function.
  • Incorporate rosemary into your cooking to add flavor and benefit from its antioxidant properties.

Chamomile

How to Use:

  • Drink chamomile tea before bed to promote relaxation and improve sleep quality.
  • Apply chamomile tea bags on the eyes to reduce puffiness and dark circles.
  • Use chamomile oil for a calming massage to relieve muscle tension.

Lemongrass

How to Use:

  • Make lemongrass tea to aid digestion and relieve anxiety.
  • Apply lemongrass oil to skin to treat acne and reduce inflammation.
  • Incorporate lemongrass into soups and curries for flavor and digestive benefits.
